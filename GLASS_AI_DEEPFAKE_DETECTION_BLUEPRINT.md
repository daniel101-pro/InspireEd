## STEP 1 — GLOBAL RESEARCH

### 1A. AI Video Generation Technologies: Internal Mechanics & Weaknesses

#### 1A.1 GAN-Based Video Generators

**Architecture**: GAN video generators extend the adversarial framework (Generator G vs. Discriminator D) to the spatiotemporal domain. Key architectures include:

- **VideoGAN / TGAN**: Uses a 3D deconvolutional generator that takes a latent vector z ~ N(0, I) and produces a tensor of shape (T, H, W, C). The discriminator operates on 3D convolutions across the spatiotemporal volume.
- **MoCoGAN (Motion and Content Decomposed GAN)**: Separates content code z_C (fixed per video) from motion codes z_M^(t) (varying per frame). The motion is generated by an RNN: z_M^(t) = GRU(z_M^(t-1), ε_t). This decomposition means G_I(z_C, z_M^(t)) produces each frame.
- **DVD-GAN**: Operates at dual scales — a spatial discriminator D_S evaluates individual frames while a temporal discriminator D_T evaluates fixed-resolution downsampled video. Uses spectral normalization and class-conditional batch normalization.
- **StyleGAN-V**: Extends StyleGAN3 to video by treating time as a continuous signal. Maps latent codes through a mapping network to style vectors that modulate synthesis layers, with a motion generator producing time-dependent displacements.

**Internal Weaknesses**:
1. **Mode collapse in temporal domain**: GANs struggle to maintain identity consistency over long sequences because the discriminator cannot effectively supervise long-range temporal coherence.
2. **Checkerboard artifacts**: Transposed convolutions with misaligned stride/kernel size produce periodic patterns in frequency space at f = f_Nyquist / stride.
3. **Spectral decay**: GAN generators cannot reproduce the full frequency spectrum of natural video. The power spectral density P(f) of GAN-generated frames falls off faster than the 1/f^β distribution of natural images (typically β_GAN ≈ 2.5 vs. β_real ≈ 2.0).
4. **Temporal aliasing**: Frame-by-frame generation or RNN-based motion produces subtle jitter because the motion model lacks true physics-based kinematics.
5. **Boundary artifacts**: Face swap GANs produce a "blending boundary" where the synthesized face region meets the original background, detectable via gradient magnitude analysis.

#### 1A.2 Diffusion-Based Video Models

**Architecture**: Video diffusion models extend DDPM/DDIM to spatiotemporal tensors:

- **Forward process**: q(x_t | x_{t-1}) = N(x_t; √(1-β_t) x_{t-1}, β_t I), progressively adding noise over T steps to a video tensor x_0 ∈ R^(F×H×W×C).
- **Reverse process**: p_θ(x_{t-1} | x_t) = N(x_{t-1}; μ_θ(x_t, t), Σ_θ(x_t, t)), where μ_θ is predicted by a 3D U-Net with temporal attention layers.
- **Latent diffusion (Stable Video Diffusion)**: Operates in a compressed latent space z = E(x) using a pretrained VAE encoder E and decoder D. The diffusion operates on z ∈ R^(F×h×w×c) where h,w << H,W.
- **Sora-class models**: Use a Diffusion Transformer (DiT) backbone operating on spacetime patches. Video is patchified into tokens of size (t_p, h_p, w_p), processed by transformer blocks with full spatiotemporal attention.

**Internal Weaknesses**:
1. **VAE reconstruction artifacts**: The encode-decode cycle through the VAE introduces subtle blurring and color shifting, particularly in high-frequency details. The VAE's KL regularization (D_KL(q(z|x) || p(z))) causes information loss.
2. **Temporal attention limitations**: Even with temporal attention layers, maintaining coherence beyond ~4-8 seconds remains difficult. Autoregressive extension (generating future chunks conditioned on past) introduces "drift" — gradual quality and consistency degradation.
3. **Noise schedule fingerprint**: The specific noise schedule β_t used during training leaves a statistical signature in the generated output. The residual noise pattern after generation is not truly random but follows the learned denoising distribution.
4. **Classifier-free guidance artifacts**: CFG at high scales (w > 7.5) produces oversaturated colors and exaggerated contrast, computable as: x_guided = x_uncond + w(x_cond - x_uncond).
5. **Patch boundary artifacts**: DiT-based architectures can produce subtle discontinuities at patch boundaries due to the limited receptive field of local attention in early layers.

#### 1A.3 Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) Video Synthesis

**Architecture**: NeRF represents a scene as a continuous function F_θ: (x, y, z, θ, φ) → (r, g, b, σ), mapping 3D position and viewing direction to color and density. Video extensions include:

- **D-NeRF / Nerfies**: Add a deformation field D_ψ(x, t) → Δx that warps canonical-space points based on time, enabling dynamic scene reconstruction.
- **Neural Volumes**: Represent scenes as 3D feature volumes decoded by a neural network, rasterized via differentiable ray marching.
- **HyperNeRF**: Uses higher-dimensional ambient spaces to handle topological changes (mouth opening, etc.).

**Internal Weaknesses**:
1. **View-dependent artifacts**: NeRFs trained from limited viewpoints produce "floaters" — semi-transparent density blobs visible from novel angles.
2. **Temporal deformation artifacts**: The deformation field D_ψ cannot perfectly model complex non-rigid motions like cloth dynamics or hair movement, producing "ghosting" near fast-moving boundaries.
3. **Rendering equation violations**: NeRF's volumetric rendering C(r) = ∫T(t)σ(r(t))c(r(t),d)dt does not model interreflections, subsurface scattering, or caustics, making materials like skin appear too uniform.
4. **Resolution-dependent hash collisions**: Instant-NGP style NeRFs using hash-based feature grids produce subtle aliasing at hash collision boundaries.

#### 1A.4 Text-to-Video Models

**Architecture**: Text-to-video systems combine language understanding with video generation:

- **Pipeline**: Text → CLIP/T5 encoding → Cross-attention in diffusion model → Video latents → VAE decode → Pixel video
- **Key models**: Runway Gen-3, Sora, Kling, Pika, Luma Dream Machine
- **Conditioning**: Text embeddings are injected via cross-attention: Attention(Q_video, K_text, V_text) at each denoising step

**Internal Weaknesses**:
1. **Semantic-visual misalignment**: Complex prompts produce physically impossible arrangements — hands with wrong digit count, text rendering failures, impossible object interactions.
2. **Physics violations**: Generated videos routinely violate Newtonian mechanics — objects float, shadows move independently, liquids behave incorrectly.
3. **Geometric inconsistency**: 3D structure is only approximate. Moving camera reveals that objects lack consistent 3D geometry — surfaces warp and deform.
4. **Texture flickering**: Frame-to-frame texture on surfaces (especially text, patterns, fine details) is temporally unstable because each denoising step introduces independent noise.

#### 1A.5 Face Swap Pipelines

**Architecture**: Modern face swap follows this pipeline:

```
Source face → Face detection → Face alignment → Feature extraction
Target video → Face detection → Face alignment → Feature extraction
Face feature transfer → Face generation → Face blending → Output
```

Key implementations:
- **DeepFaceLab**: Encoder-decoder with shared encoder. E(face_src) → latent → D_dst(latent) → swapped face. The shared encoder forces both faces into a common latent space.
- **FaceShifter**: Two-stage architecture — (1) Adaptive Embedding Integration Network (AEI-Net) generates the swapped face, (2) Heuristic Error Acknowledging Refinement Network (HEAR-Net) corrects artifacts.
- **SimSwap**: Uses an ID injection module that injects source identity features into the generator while preserving target attributes (pose, expression, lighting).

**Internal Weaknesses**:
1. **Blending boundary**: The alpha-blended boundary between swapped face and original skin produces detectable gradient discontinuities. The Laplacian ∇²I at the boundary differs from natural skin.
2. **Illumination mismatch**: The swapped face inherits the source's implicit illumination model, creating inconsistencies with the target's environment lighting detectable via Spherical Harmonic decomposition.
3. **Resolution mismatch**: Face generators typically operate at fixed resolution (256² or 512²), then upscale and blend into potentially higher-resolution targets, leaving frequency-domain discontinuities.
4. **Expression transfer artifacts**: Mapping expressions between geometrically different faces produces unnatural deformations, especially around the mouth, jawline, and nasolabial folds.

#### 1A.6 Motion Transfer / Face Reenactment Systems

**Architecture**:
- **First Order Motion Model (FOMM)**: Extracts keypoints and local affine transformations from driving video, applies them to source image via a dense motion field and occlusion mask.
- **Face2Face**: Transfers facial expressions by fitting a parametric face model (3DMM) to both source and target, then transferring expression parameters: α_target_new = α_target_shape + α_source_expression.
- **PIRenderer**: Uses 3D-aware warping fields conditioned on predicted facial motion parameters (yaw, pitch, roll, expression).

**Internal Weaknesses**:
1. **Occlusion handling failure**: When the driving head turns, revealing previously occluded regions, the model must hallucinate content, producing blurry or texturally inconsistent areas.
2. **Keypoint instability**: Detected keypoints jitter between frames, causing subtle facial vibrations not present in natural video.
3. **Background warping**: Motion field application can inadvertently warp background regions near the face boundary.

#### 1A.7 Voice Cloning + Lip Synchronization

**Architecture**:
- **Voice cloning**: Speaker embedding extraction → text-to-mel-spectrogram (Tacotron/VITS) → vocoder (HiFi-GAN/WaveNet) → waveform
- **Lip sync (Wav2Lip)**: Audio → mel-spectrogram → lip sync generator that modifies mouth region of target video to match audio. Specifically: G(face_masked, mel_chunk) → face_with_synced_lips.
- **SadTalker / Audio2Head**: Audio → 3DMM expression parameters → motion field → animated face

**Internal Weaknesses**:
1. **Formant irregularity**: Cloned voices show unnatural formant transitions and F0 contour patterns detectable via MFCC analysis.
2. **Lip-jaw decoupling**: Synthetic lip movements lack the natural biomechanical coupling between jaw movement, lip shape, and tongue position.
3. **Breathing artifacts**: AI voice lacks natural breathing patterns — inter-utterance breaths, micro-pauses, and glottal stops are missing or incorrectly timed.
4. **Mouth interior**: Lip sync generators poorly render teeth, tongue, and mouth interior, often producing blurry or unrealistic oral cavity regions.

### 1B. Comprehensive Artifact Taxonomy

#### 1B.1 Face and Identity Artifacts

| Artifact | Detection Method | Frequency |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Asymmetrical facial geometry | Compare L/R facial landmark distances: d_asym = |dist(L_i, center) - dist(R_i, center)| | High in face swaps |
| Unstable facial landmarks | Compute landmark jitter: J = Σ||L_i(t) - L_i(t-1) - flow(L_i(t-1))|| | High in reenactment |
| Identity drift across frames | Track ArcFace embeddings over time: drift = 1 - cos(e(t), e(0)) | Medium in long sequences |
| Unnatural micro-expressions | FACS Action Unit temporal analysis — AU combinations violating Emotional FACS rules | Low but specific |
| Ear/hair boundary inconsistency | Segmentation boundary analysis at face/hair/ear interfaces | High in face swaps |

#### 1B.2 Eye Behavior Anomalies

- **Abnormal blinking**: Real blink duration 100-400ms, frequency 15-20/min. Many deepfakes show reduced blink rate (< 5/min) or missing blinks entirely because training data is biased toward open-eye frames.
- **Gaze inconsistencies**: Saccadic eye movements follow the main sequence relationship: peak_velocity = 600 × (1 - e^(-amplitude/12)). Synthetic gaze lacks these kinematics.
- **Corneal specular reflections**: Real eyes show consistent specular highlights reflecting the environment. Both eyes should show geometrically consistent reflections. Inconsistent or missing reflections indicate synthesis.
- **Pupil dynamics**: Real pupils dilate/contract with ~200ms latency to lighting changes (pupillary light reflex). Synthetic faces lack this physiological response.
- **Iris texture**: Real irises have unique crypts, furrows, and collarette structure. GAN-generated irises show unrealistic radial patterns.

#### 1B.3 Temporal Artifacts

- **Frame-to-frame inconsistencies**: Optical flow fields in real video are smooth and physically consistent. Synthetic video shows flow discontinuities at generation boundaries: ||∇(flow_pred - flow_actual)||₂ > threshold.
- **Temporal flickering**: High-frequency intensity oscillation in synthetic video measurable as: flicker(x,y) = var(I(x,y,t)) - E[var(I(x±δ, y±δ, t))] (pixel intensity variance exceeds local spatial variance).
- **Inconsistent motion vectors**: H.264/H.265 encoded real video has predictable motion vector distributions. Synthetic video re-encoded produces anomalous MV patterns because the underlying motion doesn't follow real physics.
- **Identity drift over time**: Face identity vector e(t) should remain constant for a single person. Plotting ||e(t) - e(0)||₂ over time reveals linear drift in synthetic video vs. bounded random walk in real video.
- **Temporal frequency anomalies**: Real video has continuous temporal frequency content. Frame-by-frame generated video shows spectral peaks at the generation framerate with missing intermediate frequencies.

#### 1B.4 Lighting Inconsistencies

- **Shadow direction mismatch**: Estimate light direction from facial shadows using Shape-from-Shading. Compute Spherical Harmonic coefficients L_lm for different facial regions — they should be consistent across the face.
- **Specular highlight inconsistency**: Highlights on skin, eyes, and nearby objects should imply the same light source positions. Compute inconsistency: C = Σ||L_estimated(region_i) - L_estimated(region_j)||².
- **Global illumination mismatch**: Environment lighting estimated from face should match background lighting. Use neural inverse rendering to estimate environment maps from face vs. scene and compare.
- **Color temperature inconsistency**: Different regions of a composited deepfake may show different white balance / color temperature characteristics.

#### 1B.5 Skin Texture Anomalies

- **Over-smoothing**: Real skin at macro scale shows pores, fine wrinkles, and sebaceous texture. GAN/diffusion outputs often over-smooth skin. Detect via: high_freq_energy = Σ|FFT(patch)|² for f > f_threshold. Synthetic skin shows lower high-frequency energy.
- **GAN texture noise**: GAN generators produce characteristic noise patterns related to their architecture. Extract via: noise_residual = image - denoise(image). The noise residual of GAN images shows periodic patterns.
- **Frequency spectrum anomalies**: The azimuthally averaged power spectrum of natural images follows P(f) ∝ 1/f^β with β ≈ 2.0. GAN images show deviations, especially at high frequencies corresponding to upsampling artifacts.

#### 1B.6 Geometry Inconsistencies

- **Head pose mismatch**: Estimated head pose (yaw, pitch, roll) from 2D landmarks vs. 3D model fitting should agree. Face swaps often show disagreement because the 3D structure of the swapped face doesn't match the target's head geometry.
- **Depth map inconsistencies**: Monocular depth estimation of real faces produces smooth, anatomically correct depth maps. Deepfake faces produce noisy or incorrect depth, especially at boundaries.
- **Perspective distortion**: A single camera produces consistent perspective projection across the frame. Composited elements may show inconsistent vanishing points.

#### 1B.7 Compression Artifacts Unique to Synthetic Videos

- **Double compression signatures**: Real video captured and shared undergoes characteristic compression. Deepfakes undergo additional encode-decode cycles, producing nested quantization artifacts detectable via DCT coefficient histogram analysis.
- **I-frame / P-frame inconsistency**: Face manipulation applied to decoded frames and re-encoded produces different quantization patterns in manipulated vs. unmanipulated regions across I-frames and P-frames.
- **Quantization table fingerprints**: Different encoding pipelines use different quantization tables. Inconsistencies within a single video reveal compositing.

#### 1B.8 GAN Fingerprints

GANs leave distinctive frequency-domain fingerprints due to their architecture:

1. **Upsampling fingerprints**: Transposed convolutions produce periodic patterns at frequencies f = k × f_s / stride where f_s is the spatial frequency. These "spectral peaks" are visible in the 2D FFT as bright spots at regular intervals.
2. **Architecture-specific patterns**: Each GAN architecture (StyleGAN, ProGAN, BigGAN) produces unique spectral signatures because their upsampling layers, normalization methods, and activation functions create specific frequency artifacts.
3. **Training set fingerprint**: GANs subtly memorize statistical properties of their training data, producing outputs whose higher-order statistics (kurtosis, skewness of DCT coefficients) match training data rather than the depicted scene.

**Detection**: Compute the 2D DFT magnitude spectrum |F(u,v)|, azimuthally average to get P(f), and analyze deviations from natural 1/f^β distribution. Apply a learned classifier to the spectral representation.

#### 1B.9 Diffusion Model Fingerprints

Diffusion models leave different but equally detectable fingerprints:

1. **Denoising schedule residuals**: The specific noise schedule β_t and number of denoising steps T leave a statistical signature. With fewer steps (DDIM with 20 steps), the output retains subtle structured noise patterns corresponding to the noise prediction error at the final step.
2. **VAE reconstruction fingerprint**: Latent diffusion models encode through a VAE whose decoder has fixed architecture. The decoder's transposed convolutions leave frequency-domain artifacts similar to GANs but at different characteristic frequencies determined by the VAE's downsampling factor (typically 8x).
3. **Guidance artifacts**: Classifier-free guidance creates contrast and saturation patterns. The ratio of guided vs. unguided predictions leaves a detectable statistical signature in color channel histograms.
4. **Quantization of latent space**: The discrete nature of floating-point latent representations creates subtle banding in smooth gradient regions.

### 1C. Audio-Visual Inconsistency Detection

#### 1C.1 Lip Sync Mismatch

**Detection methodology**:

1. **Phoneme-viseme mapping**: Extract phonemes from audio using ASR and map to expected viseme sequences. Compare expected mouth shapes against detected mouth shapes using landmark analysis:
   - Bilabial consonants (p, b, m) → lips should close completely
   - Labiodental (f, v) → lower lip contacts upper teeth
   - Open vowels (a) → wide mouth opening
   - Compute viseme_error = Σ ||viseme_expected(t) - viseme_detected(t)||₂

2. **Temporal alignment**: Measure audio-visual synchrony using SyncNet-style analysis. Extract audio features (mel-spectrogram) and visual features (mouth region embeddings) and compute cross-modal correlation. Real video shows tight synchrony (< 40ms offset). Deepfakes often show > 80ms systematic offset.

3. **Coarticulation modeling**: Real speech exhibits coarticulation — mouth shapes are influenced by surrounding phonemes. Current lip-sync generators handle each phoneme independently, missing transitions like the rounded lips on /s/ before /u/.

#### 1C.2 Phoneme-Mouth Shape Mismatch

Build a classifier that takes (audio_segment, mouth_crop) pairs and predicts whether they're genuinely synchronized:

```
Features:
- Mouth aspect ratio: MAR = (||p2-p6|| + ||p3-p5||) / (2 × ||p1-p4||)
- Lip aperture area from landmarks
- Mel-spectrogram energy in formant bands
- Delta features (rate of change of above)
```

#### 1C.3 Speech Timing Analysis

- **Voice Activity Detection alignment**: VAD from audio should match detected mouth movement. Compute: timing_error = |onset_audio - onset_visual|.
- **Syllable rate consistency**: Natural speech in English averages 4-5 syllables/second. Measure whether mouth movement frequency matches syllable rate.
- **Pause behavior**: Natural speakers show correlated audio pauses and mouth closure. Deepfakes may show mouth movement during audio silence or vice versa.

#### 1C.4 Breathing Pattern Analysis

- **Respiratory rhythm**: Normal breathing rate is 12-20 breaths/min. Detect chest/shoulder micro-movements at this frequency in real video. Voice cloned audio lacks corresponding physical breathing signals.
- **Breath sounds**: Real speech contains audible inhalation/exhalation between phrases. Analyze spectrogram for breath noise signatures (broadband noise bursts at 0.3-1s duration).

### 1D. Biological Realism Detection

#### 1D.1 Natural Muscle Movement

Real facial expressions involve coordinated activation of facial muscles (Action Units in FACS):

- **Duchenne smile**: Genuine smiles activate both zygomaticus major (AU12) AND orbicularis oculi (AU6). Fake smiles lack AU6 (eye crinkle). Many deepfakes produce smiles without proper AU6 activation.
- **Temporal dynamics**: Real muscle activation follows biomechanical curves — fast onset (ballistic phase), sustained activation, then gradual relaxation. Deepfakes often show linear interpolation between expression states.
- **Asymmetric activation**: Real expressions show slight natural asymmetry (right hemiface is typically more expressive). Perfect bilateral symmetry suggests synthesis.

**Detection**: Train an AU detector and analyze temporal AU activation patterns against a database of biomechanically valid patterns.

#### 1D.2 Biological Micro-Motions

- **Physiological tremor**: All humans exhibit involuntary micro-tremor at 8-12 Hz (physiological tremor). This affects head position, causing micro-oscillations of ~0.1-0.3° in head pose. Detectable via high-precision landmark tracking and FFT analysis.
- **Vestibulo-ocular reflex (VOR)**: When the head moves, eyes compensate with counter-rotation (gain ≈ 1.0). In deepfakes, head and eye movements may be independently generated, violating VOR.
- **Micro-saccades**: During fixation, eyes make tiny involuntary movements (microsaccades, drift, tremor). These follow specific kinematic patterns that synthetic eye models don't reproduce.

#### 1D.3 Blood Flow (Remote Photoplethysmography — rPPG)

Real human skin shows subtle color variations synchronized with heartbeat (0.7-3 Hz, corresponding to 42-180 BPM):

- **Detection method**: Apply chrominance-based rPPG (CHROM algorithm) or POS (Plane Orthogonal to Skin) to extract pulse signal from facial video.
  ```
  S(t) = X(t)/μ_x - Y(t)/μ_y  where X = R-G, Y = 0.5R + 0.5G - B
  ```
- **Pulse signal characteristics**: Real video produces a clear periodic signal with identifiable systolic peaks. Synthetic faces produce noise or no signal.
- **Spatial consistency**: Pulse signals from different facial ROIs (forehead, left cheek, right cheek) should show the same heart rate with predictable phase delays (Pulse Transit Time). Inconsistency indicates synthesis.

#### 1D.4 Subtle Facial Tremors

- **Fasciculation**: Involuntary muscle fiber twitching visible in high-resolution video, particularly in periorbital and perioral regions.
- **Frequency analysis**: Real facial tremor has characteristic frequency content (8-12 Hz band). Apply STFTs to facial landmark trajectories and check for physiological tremor signature.

### 1E. Physical World Constraint Violations

#### 1E.1 Impossible Lighting

- **Multiple light source detection**: Estimate light direction from specular highlights on eyes, nose, and shiny surfaces. All should indicate consistent light sources. Face swaps often import the source face's lighting.
  ```
  Algorithm: For each specular highlight:
    1. Estimate surface normal n from 3D face model
    2. Estimate view direction v from camera model
    3. Compute light direction: l = 2(n·v)n - v (mirror reflection)
    4. All estimated l vectors should cluster into consistent light sources
  ```

- **Shadow consistency**: Cast shadows must be geometrically consistent with estimated light positions. Verify using shadow-light source consistency checks.

#### 1E.2 Impossible Reflections

- **Eye reflection analysis**: Both eyes should reflect the same scene. The reflection position should be consistent with estimated gaze direction and the reflector's position in the scene.
- **Window/glass reflections**: Any reflective surfaces in the scene should show consistent reflections matching the 3D scene geometry.

#### 1E.3 Inconsistent Gravity Effects

- **Hair dynamics**: Hair should respond to gravity and head acceleration consistently. AI-generated hair often shows physically impossible floating or clipping.
- **Clothing dynamics**: Fabric should follow gravitational draping. Neural rendering often produces incorrect cloth behavior.
- **Object interactions**: Held objects should show consistent gravitational behavior and realistic hand-object interaction forces.

#### 1E.4 Motion Blur Mismatch

- **Exposure-consistent blur**: Real motion blur is determined by shutter speed / frame rate ratio. The blur kernel should be consistent with measured inter-frame motion:
  ```
  blur_length = velocity × exposure_time
  ```
  Synthetic video often has no motion blur (each frame rendered at instant) or incorrect blur direction/magnitude.
- **Rolling shutter**: Real CMOS sensors read line-by-line, causing characteristic skew during fast motion. AI-generated video lacks rolling shutter artifacts or has them incorrectly simulated.

### 1F. Camera Sensor Fingerprints

#### 1F.1 Photo Response Non-Uniformity (PRNU)

Every camera sensor has a unique noise pattern (PRNU fingerprint) caused by manufacturing variations in silicon:

- **Extraction**: PRNU = average over N frames of (frame - denoised_frame). The PRNU pattern K is fixed for a given sensor.
- **Detection**: Real video from the same camera shows consistent PRNU across all frames. Deepfake video has no PRNU or inconsistent PRNU between manipulated and non-manipulated regions.
- **Correlation**: For each frame, compute normalized cross-correlation between extracted noise residual and estimated camera PRNU. Real frames: ρ > 0.01, Fake regions: ρ ≈ 0.

#### 1F.2 Rolling Shutter Artifacts

Real CMOS sensors read rows sequentially, producing:
- Skew during horizontal motion
- Wobble during vibration
- Partial exposure during flash

These artifacts are physically consistent with the camera's row readout time (typically 10-30ms for consumer cameras). AI-generated video either lacks these entirely or applies them as a post-process that's inconsistent with actual sensor behavior.

#### 1F.3 Lens Distortion

Real lenses produce measurable barrel/pincushion distortion following the Brown-Conrady model:
```
x_distorted = x(1 + k₁r² + k₂r⁴ + k₃r⁶) + 2p₁xy + p₂(r² + 2x²)
y_distorted = y(1 + k₁r² + k₂r⁴ + k₃r⁶) + p₁(r² + 2y²) + 2p₂xy
```

AI-generated video typically shows perfect perspective projection (no distortion) or inconsistent distortion across the frame, as generative models are trained on undistorted images.

#### 1F.4 Chromatic Aberration

Real lenses produce wavelength-dependent magnification, causing color fringing at frame edges:
- **Lateral chromatic aberration**: R, G, B channels have slightly different magnifications
- **Detection**: Measure per-channel edge positions at frame periphery. Real video shows systematic, radially-dependent color fringing. AI video shows none or inconsistent patterns.

#### 1F.5 Sensor Noise Characteristics

Real camera sensors produce noise following specific distributions:
- **Shot noise**: Poisson distributed, signal-dependent: σ² = α × I
- **Read noise**: Gaussian, signal-independent: N(0, σ_read²)
- **Fixed pattern noise**: Spatially fixed, frame-independent

AI-generated video noise is typically i.i.d. Gaussian or absent, violating these physical noise models. Fit a heteroscedastic noise model σ²(I) = αI + β to local patches. Real video follows this model; synthetic video shows deviations.

### 3.3 Module 1: Spatial Artifact Detection (Vision Transformer)

```python
class SpatialArtifactDetector(nn.Module):
    """
    Detects per-frame spatial artifacts using a Vision Transformer backbone
    with specialized heads for different artifact types.

    Architecture: ViT-L/14 (pretrained on CLIP) with custom detection heads
    Input: Face crops (224×224) or full frames
    Output: Spatial artifact feature vector (d=1024)
    """

    def __init__(self):
        super().__init__()
        # Backbone: ViT-L/14 pretrained, fine-tuned
        self.backbone = ViT_L_14(pretrained="clip", frozen_layers=12)
        # Output: [CLS] token + patch tokens (196 patches of 16×16)

        # Head 1: Binary authenticity classifier
        self.authenticity_head = nn.Sequential(
            nn.LayerNorm(1024),
            nn.Linear(1024, 512),
            nn.GELU(),
            nn.Dropout(0.1),
            nn.Linear(512, 2)  # [real, fake]
        )

        # Head 2: Patch-level manipulation map
        self.manipulation_map_head = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Conv1d(1024, 256, 1),
            nn.GELU(),
            nn.Conv1d(256, 1, 1),
            nn.Sigmoid()
        )  # Output: per-patch manipulation probability → reshape to 14×14 map

        # Head 3: Artifact type classifier
        self.artifact_head = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(1024, 512),
            nn.GELU(),
            nn.Linear(512, NUM_ARTIFACT_TYPES)  # multi-label
        )

        # Auxiliary: Frequency-aware attention
        self.freq_attention = FrequencyAttentionModule(d_model=1024)

    def forward(self, face_crops):
        """
        face_crops: (B, C, H, W) = (B, 3, 224, 224)
        """
        # Extract ViT features
        cls_token, patch_tokens = self.backbone(face_crops)
        # cls_token: (B, 1024), patch_tokens: (B, 196, 1024)

        # Apply frequency-aware attention (emphasize high-freq patches)
        patch_tokens = self.freq_attention(patch_tokens, face_crops)

        # Aggregate with attention pooling
        spatial_features = self.attention_pool(cls_token, patch_tokens)

        # Predictions
        authenticity = self.authenticity_head(spatial_features)
        manip_map = self.manipulation_map_head(patch_tokens.transpose(1,2))
        manip_map = manip_map.reshape(-1, 14, 14)  # spatial map
        artifact_types = self.artifact_head(spatial_features)

        return {
            'features': spatial_features,            # (B, 1024)
            'authenticity': authenticity,             # (B, 2)
            'manipulation_map': manip_map,           # (B, 14, 14)
            'artifact_types': torch.sigmoid(artifact_types)  # (B, N_artifacts)
        }


class FrequencyAttentionModule(nn.Module):
    """
    Applies frequency-domain analysis to guide spatial attention.
    GAN/diffusion artifacts are often more visible in frequency domain.
    """

    def __init__(self, d_model):
        super().__init__()
        self.freq_encoder = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(d_model, d_model),
            nn.GELU(),
            nn.Linear(d_model, d_model)
        )
        self.gate = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(d_model * 2, d_model),
            nn.Sigmoid()
        )

    def forward(self, patch_tokens, images):
        """
        Compute FFT of input image patches and combine with spatial features
        """
        B, N, D = patch_tokens.shape

        # Compute 2D FFT of image patches
        patches = images.unfold(2, 16, 16).unfold(3, 16, 16)  # (B, C, 14, 14, 16, 16)
        patches = patches.reshape(B, 3, N, 16, 16)

        # FFT magnitude spectrum per patch
        fft_mag = torch.fft.fft2(patches).abs()
        fft_features = fft_mag.reshape(B, N, -1)  # (B, N, 3*16*16)
        fft_features = nn.Linear(3*16*16, D)(fft_features)

        # Frequency-guided gating
        freq_encoded = self.freq_encoder(fft_features)
        gate = self.gate(torch.cat([patch_tokens, freq_encoded], dim=-1))

        return patch_tokens * gate + freq_encoded * (1 - gate)
```

### 3.4 Module 2: CNN Frequency Artifact Extractor

```python
class FrequencyArtifactCNN(nn.Module):
    """
    Specialized CNN for detecting frequency-domain artifacts.
    Uses constrained first-layer filters (SRM-style) to extract noise residuals.

    Inspired by: SRM filters (Steganalysis Rich Model), F3-Net architecture
    Input: Face crops or full frames at original resolution
    Output: Frequency artifact features (d=512)
    """

    def __init__(self):
        super().__init__()

        # Layer 0: SRM-style constrained convolutional filters
        # These high-pass filters suppress image content and expose noise/artifacts
        self.srm_conv = nn.Conv2d(3, 30, kernel_size=5, padding=2, bias=False)
        self.srm_conv.weight = nn.Parameter(self._get_srm_filters(), requires_grad=False)

        # Frequency stream: operates on FFT magnitude
        self.freq_stream = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Conv2d(6, 64, 3, padding=1),   # 6 channels: mag + phase for RGB
            nn.BatchNorm2d(64),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Conv2d(64, 128, 3, stride=2, padding=1),
            nn.BatchNorm2d(128),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Conv2d(128, 256, 3, stride=2, padding=1),
            nn.BatchNorm2d(256),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.AdaptiveAvgPool2d(1)
        )

        # Noise residual stream: operates on denoised residuals
        self.noise_stream = EfficientNet_B0(in_channels=30, num_classes=256)

        # DCT coefficient stream
        self.dct_stream = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Conv2d(192, 128, 1),  # 64 DCT coefficients × 3 channels
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Conv2d(128, 256, 3, stride=2, padding=1),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.AdaptiveAvgPool2d(1),
            nn.Flatten(),
            nn.Linear(256, 256)
        )

        # Fusion
        self.fusion = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(256 + 256 + 256, 512),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Dropout(0.2),
            nn.Linear(512, 512)
        )

    def forward(self, images):
        """images: (B, 3, H, W) at original resolution"""

        # Stream 1: Noise residual analysis
        noise_residual = self.srm_conv(images)  # (B, 30, H, W)
        noise_features = self.noise_stream(noise_residual)  # (B, 256)

        # Stream 2: Frequency spectrum analysis
        fft = torch.fft.fft2(images)
        fft_mag = torch.log1p(fft.abs())  # (B, 3, H, W)
        fft_phase = fft.angle()            # (B, 3, H, W)
        freq_input = torch.cat([fft_mag, fft_phase], dim=1)  # (B, 6, H, W)
        freq_features = self.freq_stream(freq_input).squeeze(-1).squeeze(-1)  # (B, 256)

        # Stream 3: DCT coefficient analysis
        dct_coeffs = self._block_dct(images, block_size=8)  # (B, 192, H/8, W/8)
        dct_features = self.dct_stream(dct_coeffs)  # (B, 256)

        # Fuse all frequency features
        combined = torch.cat([noise_features, freq_features, dct_features], dim=1)
        return self.fusion(combined)  # (B, 512)

    def _get_srm_filters(self):
        """Initialize with Steganalysis Rich Model high-pass filters"""
        # 30 SRM filters that extract noise residuals
        # Includes edge detectors, Laplacian, and higher-order filters
        filters = torch.zeros(30, 3, 5, 5)
        # ... (standard SRM filter initialization)
        return filters

    def _block_dct(self, images, block_size=8):
        """Compute block DCT coefficients (similar to JPEG)"""
        B, C, H, W = images.shape
        # Reshape into 8×8 blocks and apply DCT
        blocks = images.unfold(2, block_size, block_size).unfold(3, block_size, block_size)
        dct_blocks = torch.fft.dctn(blocks, dim=[-2, -1], norm='ortho')
        # Flatten coefficient indices
        return dct_blocks.reshape(B, C * block_size * block_size, H // block_size, W // block_size)
```

### 3.5 Module 3: Temporal Transformer

```python
class TemporalConsistencyTransformer(nn.Module):
    """
    Analyzes temporal consistency across video frames.
    Detects: identity drift, temporal flickering, motion inconsistencies,
    unnatural transitions, missing biological signals.

    Architecture: TimeSformer-style divided space-time attention
    Input: Sequence of frame features + raw optical flow
    Output: Temporal consistency features (d=768)
    """

    def __init__(self, d_model=768, n_heads=12, n_layers=8, max_frames=64):
        super().__init__()

        # Temporal position embedding
        self.temporal_pos_embed = nn.Parameter(torch.randn(1, max_frames, d_model))

        # Frame feature projector (from spatial backbone)
        self.frame_projector = nn.Linear(1024, d_model)

        # Optical flow encoder
        self.flow_encoder = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Conv2d(2, 64, 3, stride=2, padding=1),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Conv2d(64, 128, 3, stride=2, padding=1),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.AdaptiveAvgPool2d(1),
            nn.Flatten(),
            nn.Linear(128, d_model)
        )

        # Temporal transformer blocks
        self.transformer_blocks = nn.ModuleList([
            TemporalTransformerBlock(d_model, n_heads) for _ in range(n_layers)
        ])

        # Specialized temporal analysis heads
        self.identity_drift_head = nn.Linear(d_model, 1)       # scalar drift score
        self.temporal_coherence_head = nn.Linear(d_model, 1)    # coherence score
        self.motion_realism_head = nn.Linear(d_model, 1)        # motion naturalness
        self.rppg_head = rPPGExtractionHead(d_model)            # pulse signal

        # Aggregation
        self.temporal_cls = nn.Parameter(torch.randn(1, 1, d_model))
        self.output_proj = nn.Linear(d_model, 768)

    def forward(self, frame_features, optical_flows, face_crops_sequence):
        """
        frame_features: (B, T, 1024) from spatial backbone
        optical_flows: (B, T-1, 2, H, W) pre-computed flows
        face_crops_sequence: (B, T, 3, 224, 224) for rPPG
        """
        B, T, _ = frame_features.shape

        # Project frame features
        tokens = self.frame_projector(frame_features)  # (B, T, 768)

        # Encode optical flow and add to tokens
        flow_features = []
        for t in range(T - 1):
            flow_feat = self.flow_encoder(optical_flows[:, t])
            flow_features.append(flow_feat)
        flow_features.append(torch.zeros_like(flow_features[0]))  # padding
        flow_tokens = torch.stack(flow_features, dim=1)  # (B, T, 768)

        tokens = tokens + flow_tokens  # combine spatial + motion info

        # Add temporal position embeddings
        tokens = tokens + self.temporal_pos_embed[:, :T, :]

        # Prepend [CLS] token for aggregation
        cls = self.temporal_cls.expand(B, -1, -1)
        tokens = torch.cat([cls, tokens], dim=1)  # (B, T+1, 768)

        # Apply temporal transformer
        for block in self.transformer_blocks:
            tokens = block(tokens)

        cls_output = tokens[:, 0, :]        # (B, 768)
        frame_outputs = tokens[:, 1:, :]    # (B, T, 768)

        # Specialized analyses
        # Identity drift: measure embedding consistency over time
        identity_drift = self.identity_drift_head(frame_outputs).squeeze(-1)  # (B, T)

        # Temporal coherence
        coherence = self.temporal_coherence_head(cls_output)  # (B, 1)

        # Motion realism
        motion_score = self.motion_realism_head(cls_output)  # (B, 1)

        # rPPG extraction (biological signal)
        rppg_signal = self.rppg_head(face_crops_sequence)  # (B, T)

        temporal_features = self.output_proj(cls_output)  # (B, 768)

        return {
            'features': temporal_features,
            'identity_drift': identity_drift,
            'coherence': coherence,
            'motion_score': motion_score,
            'rppg_signal': rppg_signal,
            'frame_features': frame_outputs
        }


class rPPGExtractionHead(nn.Module):
    """Extract remote photoplethysmography signal to verify biological presence"""

    def __init__(self, d_model):
        super().__init__()
        # Use CHROM-based approach with learned refinement
        self.skin_segmentor = nn.Conv2d(3, 1, 1)  # learn skin mask
        self.signal_refiner = nn.GRU(3, 64, batch_first=True, bidirectional=True)
        self.output = nn.Linear(128, 1)

    def forward(self, face_crops):
        """
        face_crops: (B, T, 3, H, W)
        Returns: rPPG signal (B, T) — should show periodic pulse for real faces
        """
        B, T, C, H, W = face_crops.shape

        # CHROM algorithm: spatial averaging in color space
        skin_mask = torch.sigmoid(self.skin_segmentor(
            face_crops.reshape(B*T, C, H, W)
        )).reshape(B, T, 1, H, W)

        # Spatially average skin pixels per frame
        masked = face_crops * skin_mask
        spatial_avg = masked.sum(dim=[-1,-2]) / (skin_mask.sum(dim=[-1,-2]) + 1e-6)
        # spatial_avg: (B, T, 3) — mean R, G, B per frame

        # Normalize
        spatial_avg = spatial_avg / (spatial_avg.mean(dim=1, keepdim=True) + 1e-6)

        # GRU refinement
        refined, _ = self.signal_refiner(spatial_avg)  # (B, T, 128)
        rppg = self.output(refined).squeeze(-1)  # (B, T)

        return rppg
```

```python
class AudioForensicsTransformer(nn.Module):
    """
    Analyzes audio track for voice synthesis artifacts.
    Detects: voice cloning, TTS artifacts, splicing, environmental inconsistencies.

    Architecture: Whisper encoder (frozen) + trainable forensics heads
    Input: Audio waveform at 16kHz
    Output: Audio forensics features (d=512)
    """

    def __init__(self):
        super().__init__()

        # Backbone: Whisper-medium encoder (frozen, used as feature extractor)
        self.whisper_encoder = WhisperEncoder.from_pretrained("medium")
        self.whisper_encoder.requires_grad_(False)
        # Output: (B, T_audio, 1024)

        # Trainable audio forensics module
        self.forensics_transformer = nn.TransformerEncoder(
            nn.TransformerEncoderLayer(d_model=1024, nhead=16, dim_feedforward=2048),
            num_layers=4
        )

        # MFCC-based artifact detector (parallel stream)
        self.mfcc_cnn = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Conv2d(1, 32, (3, 3), padding=1),
            nn.BatchNorm2d(32),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Conv2d(32, 64, (3, 3), stride=(1, 2), padding=1),
            nn.BatchNorm2d(64),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Conv2d(64, 128, (3, 3), stride=(1, 2), padding=1),
            nn.BatchNorm2d(128),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.AdaptiveAvgPool2d((1, 1)),
            nn.Flatten(),
            nn.Linear(128, 256)
        )

        # Voice naturalness analysis
        self.voice_head = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(1024, 512),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Linear(512, 3)  # [real_voice, cloned_voice, tts_voice]
        )

        # Phoneme timing analyzer
        self.phoneme_analyzer = PhonemeTiming Analyzer(d_model=1024)

        # Output projection
        self.output_proj = nn.Linear(1024 + 256, 512)

    def forward(self, audio_wav, mel_spec):
        """
        audio_wav: (B, num_samples) at 16kHz
        mel_spec: (B, 80, T_mel) precomputed mel spectrogram
        """
        # Whisper features
        whisper_features = self.whisper_encoder(audio_wav)  # (B, T, 1024)
        forensics_features = self.forensics_transformer(whisper_features)
        audio_global = forensics_features.mean(dim=1)  # (B, 1024)

        # MFCC stream
        mfcc = compute_mfcc(audio_wav, n_mfcc=40)  # (B, 40, T_mfcc)
        mfcc_features = self.mfcc_cnn(mfcc.unsqueeze(1))  # (B, 256)

        # Combine
        combined = torch.cat([audio_global, mfcc_features], dim=1)
        audio_features = self.output_proj(combined)  # (B, 512)

        # Voice type classification
        voice_type = self.voice_head(audio_global)  # (B, 3)

        return {
            'features': audio_features,          # (B, 512)
            'voice_type': voice_type,             # (B, 3)
            'temporal_features': forensics_features,  # (B, T, 1024)
            'phoneme_timing': self.phoneme_analyzer(forensics_features)
        }
```

### 3.7 Module 5: Cross-Modal Fusion Transformer

```python
class CrossModalFusionTransformer(nn.Module):
    """
    Fuses spatial, temporal, frequency, and audio features with cross-attention.
    Detects inter-modal inconsistencies (e.g., lip-audio mismatch).

    Architecture: Multi-stream cross-attention transformer
    Input: Features from all modality branches
    Output: Unified detection embedding + per-modality consistency scores
    """

    def __init__(self, d_model=768, n_heads=12, n_layers=6):
        super().__init__()

        # Project all modalities to common dimension
        self.spatial_proj = nn.Linear(1024, d_model)
        self.freq_proj = nn.Linear(512, d_model)
        self.temporal_proj = nn.Linear(768, d_model)
        self.audio_proj = nn.Linear(512, d_model)
        self.metadata_proj = nn.Linear(64, d_model)

        # Modality type embeddings
        self.modality_embed = nn.Embedding(5, d_model)  # spatial, freq, temporal, audio, meta

        # Cross-modal transformer blocks
        self.fusion_layers = nn.ModuleList([
            CrossModalTransformerBlock(d_model, n_heads) for _ in range(n_layers)
        ])

        # Audio-visual sync detector (specialized)
        self.av_sync_head = AudioVisualSyncHead(d_model)

        # Global classification head
        self.classifier = nn.Sequential(
            nn.LayerNorm(d_model),
            nn.Linear(d_model, 512),
            nn.GELU(),
            nn.Dropout(0.1),
            nn.Linear(512, 256),
            nn.GELU(),
            nn.Linear(256, 4)  # [real, AI-generated, AI-modified, partially_synthetic]
        )

        # Generator identification head
        self.generator_head = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(d_model, 256),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Linear(256, NUM_KNOWN_GENERATORS)
        )

        # Confidence estimation head (learned calibration)
        self.confidence_head = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(d_model, 128),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Linear(128, 1),
            nn.Sigmoid()
        )

        # Fusion [CLS] token
        self.fusion_cls = nn.Parameter(torch.randn(1, 1, d_model))

    def forward(self, spatial_feat, freq_feat, temporal_feat, audio_feat, metadata_feat):
        """
        spatial_feat:  (B, 1024) or (B, N_patches, 1024)
        freq_feat:     (B, 512)
        temporal_feat: (B, 768) or (B, T, 768)
        audio_feat:    (B, 512) or (B, T_audio, 512)
        metadata_feat: (B, 64)
        """
        B = spatial_feat.shape[0]

        # Project to common dimension and add modality embeddings
        tokens = []

        # Handle both global and sequence features
        s = self.spatial_proj(spatial_feat)
        if s.dim() == 2: s = s.unsqueeze(1)
        s = s + self.modality_embed(torch.zeros(s.shape[1], dtype=torch.long, device=s.device))
        tokens.append(s)

        f = self.freq_proj(freq_feat).unsqueeze(1)
        f = f + self.modality_embed(torch.ones(1, dtype=torch.long, device=f.device))
        tokens.append(f)

        t = self.temporal_proj(temporal_feat)
        if t.dim() == 2: t = t.unsqueeze(1)
        t = t + self.modality_embed(2 * torch.ones(t.shape[1], dtype=torch.long, device=t.device))
        tokens.append(t)

        a = self.audio_proj(audio_feat)
        if a.dim() == 2: a = a.unsqueeze(1)
        a = a + self.modality_embed(3 * torch.ones(a.shape[1], dtype=torch.long, device=a.device))
        tokens.append(a)

        m = self.metadata_proj(metadata_feat).unsqueeze(1)
        m = m + self.modality_embed(4 * torch.ones(1, dtype=torch.long, device=m.device))
        tokens.append(m)

        # Concatenate all modality tokens
        all_tokens = torch.cat(tokens, dim=1)  # (B, N_total, d_model)

        # Prepend fusion [CLS]
        cls = self.fusion_cls.expand(B, -1, -1)
        all_tokens = torch.cat([cls, all_tokens], dim=1)

        # Apply cross-modal transformer
        for layer in self.fusion_layers:
            all_tokens = layer(all_tokens)

        fusion_embedding = all_tokens[:, 0, :]  # (B, d_model)

        # Outputs
        classification = self.classifier(fusion_embedding)      # (B, 4)
        generator_id = self.generator_head(fusion_embedding)     # (B, N_generators)
        confidence = self.confidence_head(fusion_embedding)      # (B, 1)

        # Audio-visual sync score
        av_sync = self.av_sync_head(
            visual_tokens=all_tokens[:, 1:1+s.shape[1], :],
            audio_tokens=all_tokens[:, -a.shape[1]-1:-1, :]
        )

        return {
            'classification': classification,
            'generator_id': generator_id,
            'confidence': confidence,
            'av_sync_score': av_sync,
            'fusion_embedding': fusion_embedding,
            'all_tokens': all_tokens
        }


class AudioVisualSyncHead(nn.Module):
    """Specialized head for detecting audio-visual synchronization"""

    def __init__(self, d_model):
        super().__init__()
        self.cross_attn = nn.MultiheadAttention(d_model, 8, batch_first=True)
        self.sync_classifier = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(d_model, 128),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Linear(128, 1),
            nn.Sigmoid()
        )

    def forward(self, visual_tokens, audio_tokens):
        # Cross-attend audio to visual
        attn_output, _ = self.cross_attn(audio_tokens, visual_tokens, visual_tokens)
        sync_score = self.sync_classifier(attn_output.mean(dim=1))
        return sync_score
```

```python
class BiomechanicalMotionAnalyzer(nn.Module):
    """
    Validates whether observed motion follows biomechanical constraints.
    Analyzes: facial action units, head kinematics, eye dynamics, physiological signals.

    Input: Facial landmark sequences, head pose sequences, AU predictions
    Output: Motion realism score + biological signal presence
    """

    def __init__(self, d_model=256):
        super().__init__()

        # Landmark dynamics analyzer
        self.landmark_encoder = nn.GRU(
            input_size=68*2,  # 68 landmarks × (x,y)
            hidden_size=d_model,
            num_layers=3,
            batch_first=True,
            bidirectional=True
        )

        # Head pose kinematics
        self.pose_analyzer = nn.GRU(
            input_size=6,  # yaw, pitch, roll + velocities
            hidden_size=128,
            num_layers=2,
            batch_first=True
        )

        # Action Unit temporal pattern validator
        self.au_validator = AUTemporalValidator(n_aus=17, d_model=128)

        # Blink pattern analyzer
        self.blink_analyzer = BlinkPatternAnalyzer()

        # Physiological tremor detector
        self.tremor_detector = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(d_model * 2, 128),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Linear(128, 1),
            nn.Sigmoid()
        )  # Probability that physiological tremor is present

        # Output
        self.output = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(d_model * 2 + 128 + 128 + 1, 256),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Linear(256, 128)
        )

    def forward(self, landmarks_seq, head_pose_seq, au_seq):
        """
        landmarks_seq: (B, T, 68, 2)
        head_pose_seq: (B, T, 3)  — yaw, pitch, roll
        au_seq: (B, T, 17)  — action unit intensities
        """
        B, T, _, _ = landmarks_seq.shape

        # Flatten landmarks
        lm_flat = landmarks_seq.reshape(B, T, -1)  # (B, T, 136)
        lm_features, _ = self.landmark_encoder(lm_flat)  # (B, T, 512)
        lm_global = lm_features[:, -1, :]  # (B, 512)

        # Head pose + velocity
        pose_velocity = torch.diff(head_pose_seq, dim=1, prepend=head_pose_seq[:, :1, :])
        pose_input = torch.cat([head_pose_seq, pose_velocity], dim=-1)  # (B, T, 6)
        pose_features, _ = self.pose_analyzer(pose_input)
        pose_global = pose_features[:, -1, :]  # (B, 128)

        # AU temporal validation
        au_validity = self.au_validator(au_seq)  # (B, 128)

        # Physiological tremor
        tremor_present = self.tremor_detector(lm_global)  # (B, 1)

        # Combine
        combined = torch.cat([lm_global, pose_global, au_validity, tremor_present], dim=-1)
        motion_features = self.output(combined)  # (B, 128)

        return {
            'features': motion_features,
            'tremor_present': tremor_present,
            'au_validity': au_validity,
            'blink_analysis': self.blink_analyzer(landmarks_seq)
        }
```

```python
class IdentityVerificationModule(nn.Module):
    """
    Compares detected face identity against reference footage (if available).
    Also tracks identity consistency within the video itself.

    Uses: ArcFace embeddings for identity comparison
    Detects: face swaps, identity inconsistency, morphing
    """

    def __init__(self):
        super().__init__()

        # Face recognition backbone (ArcFace ResNet-100, frozen)
        self.face_encoder = ArcFaceEncoder.from_pretrained("r100_ms1mv3")
        self.face_encoder.requires_grad_(False)

        # Identity consistency analyzer
        self.consistency_analyzer = nn.GRU(512, 256, batch_first=True, bidirectional=True)

        # Reference comparator
        self.comparator = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(512 * 2, 256),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Linear(256, 1),
            nn.Sigmoid()
        )

        # Intra-video consistency head
        self.consistency_head = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(512, 128),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Linear(128, 1),
            nn.Sigmoid()
        )

    def forward(self, face_crops_sequence, reference_images=None):
        """
        face_crops_sequence: (B, T, 3, 112, 112) — aligned face crops over time
        reference_images: (B, N_ref, 3, 112, 112) — optional reference face images
        """
        B, T = face_crops_sequence.shape[:2]

        # Extract identity embeddings for each frame
        all_crops = face_crops_sequence.reshape(B * T, 3, 112, 112)
        all_embeddings = self.face_encoder(all_crops)  # (B*T, 512)
        embeddings = all_embeddings.reshape(B, T, 512)  # (B, T, 512)

        # Intra-video identity consistency
        consistency_features, _ = self.consistency_analyzer(embeddings)
        consistency_global = consistency_features[:, -1, :]  # (B, 512)
        consistency_score = self.consistency_head(consistency_global)  # (B, 1)

        # Pairwise identity drift
        mean_embedding = embeddings.mean(dim=1, keepdim=True)  # (B, 1, 512)
        drift = 1 - F.cosine_similarity(embeddings, mean_embedding, dim=2)  # (B, T)

        result = {
            'consistency_score': consistency_score,
            'identity_drift': drift,
            'embeddings': embeddings
        }

        # Reference comparison (if available)
        if reference_images is not None:
            N_ref = reference_images.shape[1]
            ref_crops = reference_images.reshape(B * N_ref, 3, 112, 112)
            ref_embeddings = self.face_encoder(ref_crops).reshape(B, N_ref, 512)
            ref_mean = ref_embeddings.mean(dim=1)  # (B, 512)

            video_mean = embeddings.mean(dim=1)  # (B, 512)
            comparison = self.comparator(torch.cat([video_mean, ref_mean], dim=1))
            result['reference_match'] = comparison  # (B, 1)

        return result
```

```python
class FacialLandmarkExtractor:
    """
    Multi-resolution landmark extraction for geometric analysis.
    Uses MediaPipe FaceMesh (478 points) + dlib (68 points) for redundancy.
    """

    def extract(self, face_crop):
        # 68-point landmarks (dlib-compatible)
        lm68 = self.dlib_predictor(face_crop)  # (68, 2)

        # 478-point landmarks (MediaPipe FaceMesh)
        lm478 = self.mediapipe_mesh(face_crop)  # (478, 3) with depth

        # Derived geometric features
        features = {
            # Facial symmetry
            'asymmetry_score': self._compute_asymmetry(lm68),

            # Eye aspect ratio (for blink detection)
            'left_ear': self._eye_aspect_ratio(lm68[36:42]),
            'right_ear': self._eye_aspect_ratio(lm68[42:48]),

            # Mouth aspect ratio
            'mar': self._mouth_aspect_ratio(lm68[48:68]),

            # Jaw-face ratio
            'jaw_face_ratio': self._jaw_face_ratio(lm68),

            # Inter-pupillary distance stability
            'ipd': np.linalg.norm(lm68[36:42].mean(0) - lm68[42:48].mean(0)),

            # Nose-mouth-chin alignment
            'midline_deviation': self._midline_deviation(lm68),

            # 3D head pose from 478-point mesh
            'head_pose': self._estimate_head_pose(lm478),
        }
        return lm68, lm478, features

    def _compute_asymmetry(self, lm68):
        """Measure bilateral facial asymmetry"""
        midline = (lm68[27] + lm68[30] + lm68[33]) / 3  # nose bridge center

        left_dists = np.linalg.norm(lm68[:17] - midline, axis=1)   # left jaw
        right_dists = np.linalg.norm(lm68[17:27][::-1] - midline, axis=1)[:len(left_dists)]

        return np.mean(np.abs(left_dists - right_dists))

    def _eye_aspect_ratio(self, eye_points):
        """EAR = (||p2-p6|| + ||p3-p5||) / (2 × ||p1-p4||)"""
        v1 = np.linalg.norm(eye_points[1] - eye_points[5])
        v2 = np.linalg.norm(eye_points[2] - eye_points[4])
        h = np.linalg.norm(eye_points[0] - eye_points[3])
        return (v1 + v2) / (2.0 * h + 1e-6)
```

```python
class SkinMicrotextureAnalyzer:
    """
    Analyzes skin texture at multiple scales to detect over-smoothing
    and synthetic texture patterns.
    """

    def extract_features(self, face_crop, skin_mask):
        features = {}

        # 1. Local Binary Pattern (LBP) histogram
        gray = cv2.cvtColor(face_crop, cv2.COLOR_RGB2GRAY)
        lbp = local_binary_pattern(gray, P=24, R=3, method='uniform')
        features['lbp_histogram'] = np.histogram(lbp, bins=26, density=True)[0]

        # 2. Gray-Level Co-occurrence Matrix (GLCM) texture features
        glcm = graycomatrix(gray, distances=[1,2,4], angles=[0, np.pi/4, np.pi/2],
                           levels=256, symmetric=True, normed=True)
        features['glcm_contrast'] = graycoprops(glcm, 'contrast').flatten()
        features['glcm_energy'] = graycoprops(glcm, 'energy').flatten()
        features['glcm_homogeneity'] = graycoprops(glcm, 'homogeneity').flatten()
        features['glcm_correlation'] = graycoprops(glcm, 'correlation').flatten()

        # 3. High-frequency energy ratio
        fft = np.fft.fft2(gray * skin_mask)
        fft_shift = np.fft.fftshift(fft)
        magnitude = np.abs(fft_shift)

        h, w = gray.shape
        center_mask = np.zeros_like(magnitude)
        cv2.circle(center_mask, (w//2, h//2), min(h,w)//4, 1, -1)

        low_freq_energy = np.sum(magnitude * center_mask)
        high_freq_energy = np.sum(magnitude * (1 - center_mask))
        features['high_freq_ratio'] = high_freq_energy / (low_freq_energy + 1e-8)

        # 4. Pore detection score (real skin has visible pores at sufficient resolution)
        if face_crop.shape[0] >= 256:
            features['pore_score'] = self._detect_pores(gray, skin_mask)

        # 5. Gabor filter responses at multiple scales
        gabor_features = []
        for freq in [0.1, 0.2, 0.3, 0.4]:
            for theta in np.arange(0, np.pi, np.pi/4):
                kernel = cv2.getGaborKernel((21, 21), 4.0, theta, 1/freq, 0.5)
                response = cv2.filter2D(gray, cv2.CV_64F, kernel)
                gabor_features.extend([response[skin_mask > 0].mean(),
                                       response[skin_mask > 0].std()])
        features['gabor_responses'] = np.array(gabor_features)

        return features
```

```python
class GANNoiseExtractor:
    """
    Extract GAN-specific noise patterns from images.
    Real images have sensor noise; GAN images have structured generation noise.
    """

    def extract(self, image):
        """
        image: (H, W, 3) uint8
        Returns: noise_residual, spectral_features
        """
        image_f = image.astype(np.float64) / 255.0

        # 1. Denoise using wavelet-based method (BayesShrink)
        denoised = denoise_wavelet(image_f, method='BayesShrink',
                                    mode='soft', channel_axis=-1)
        noise_residual = image_f - denoised  # (H, W, 3)

        # 2. Analyze noise spectrum
        noise_fft = np.fft.fft2(noise_residual[:,:,1])  # Green channel (most info)
        noise_magnitude = np.abs(np.fft.fftshift(noise_fft))

        # 3. Azimuthal average of noise spectrum
        azimuthal_avg = self._azimuthal_average(noise_magnitude)

        # 4. Detect periodic peaks (GAN upsampling fingerprint)
        peaks, properties = find_peaks(azimuthal_avg, prominence=0.01)
        peak_features = {
            'n_peaks': len(peaks),
            'peak_positions': peaks,
            'peak_prominences': properties['prominences'],
            'peak_periodicity': np.std(np.diff(peaks)) if len(peaks) > 1 else 0
        }
```

```python
class FrequencySpectrumAnalyzer:
    """
    Comprehensive frequency-domain analysis.
    Natural images follow P(f) ∝ 1/f^β. GAN/diffusion images deviate.
    """

    def analyze(self, image):
        gray = cv2.cvtColor(image, cv2.COLOR_RGB2GRAY).astype(np.float64)

        # 1. 2D FFT magnitude spectrum
        fft2d = np.fft.fft2(gray)
        magnitude = np.abs(np.fft.fftshift(fft2d))
        log_magnitude = np.log1p(magnitude)

        # 2. Azimuthal power spectral density
        psd = self._azimuthal_psd(magnitude)

        # 3. Fit 1/f^β model
        freqs = np.arange(1, len(psd))
        log_freqs = np.log(freqs)
        log_psd = np.log(psd[1:] + 1e-10)
        beta, intercept = np.polyfit(log_freqs, log_psd, 1)
        # Real images: beta ≈ -2.0, GAN: beta ≈ -2.5 to -3.0

        # 4. Residual from 1/f^β model (reveals GAN peaks)
        fitted = intercept + beta * log_freqs
        spectral_residual = log_psd - fitted

        # 5. DCT coefficient histogram analysis
        dct = cv2.dct(gray / 255.0)
        dct_hist = np.histogram(dct.flatten(), bins=100, range=(-5, 5))[0]

        # 6. Band-pass energy distribution
        band_energies = []
        for low, high in [(0, 0.1), (0.1, 0.2), (0.2, 0.3), (0.3, 0.5), (0.5, 1.0)]:
            mask = self._bandpass_mask(gray.shape, low, high)
            band_energy = np.sum(magnitude * mask) / (np.sum(mask) + 1e-8)
            band_energies.append(band_energy)

        return {
            'beta': beta,                            # spectral slope
            'spectral_residual': spectral_residual,  # deviation from 1/f model
            'psd': psd,                              # power spectral density
            'dct_histogram': dct_hist,               # DCT coefficient distribution
            'band_energies': np.array(band_energies),# energy per frequency band
            'log_magnitude_spectrum': log_magnitude   # full 2D spectrum (for CNN)
        }
```

```python
class MotionVectorAnalyzer:
    """
    Extracts and analyzes motion vectors for temporal consistency.
    Uses both optical flow and H.264/H.265 encoded motion vectors.
    """

    def extract_optical_flow(self, frames):
        """Dense optical flow using RAFT"""
        flows = []
        for t in range(len(frames) - 1):
            flow = self.raft_model(frames[t], frames[t+1])  # (2, H, W)
            flows.append(flow)
        return np.stack(flows)  # (T-1, 2, H, W)

    def analyze_flow_consistency(self, flows, face_masks):
        """Analyze physical plausibility of motion fields"""
        features = {}

        # 1. Flow smoothness (real motion is spatially smooth)
        smoothness = []
        for flow in flows:
            grad_x = np.gradient(flow[0], axis=1)
            grad_y = np.gradient(flow[1], axis=0)
            smoothness.append(np.mean(np.abs(grad_x) + np.abs(grad_y)))
        features['flow_smoothness_mean'] = np.mean(smoothness)
        features['flow_smoothness_std'] = np.std(smoothness)

        # 2. Flow magnitude consistency (sudden jumps indicate manipulation)
        magnitudes = [np.sqrt(f[0]**2 + f[1]**2).mean() for f in flows]
        features['flow_mag_jitter'] = np.std(np.diff(magnitudes))

        # 3. Face vs background flow consistency
        for t, (flow, mask) in enumerate(zip(flows, face_masks)):
            face_flow = flow[:, mask > 0].mean(axis=1)
            bg_flow = flow[:, mask == 0].mean(axis=1)
            # In face swap, face motion may not match background
            features[f'face_bg_flow_diff_{t}'] = np.linalg.norm(face_flow - bg_flow)

        # 4. Forward-backward consistency check
        fb_errors = []
        for t in range(len(flows) - 1):
            forward = flows[t]
            backward = flows[t + 1] if t + 1 < len(flows) else flows[t]
            # Warp backward flow using forward flow
            warped_back = warp_flow(backward, forward)
            fb_error = np.mean(np.abs(forward + warped_back))
            fb_errors.append(fb_error)
        features['fb_consistency'] = np.mean(fb_errors)

        return features
```

```python
class IdentityDriftTracker:
    """
    Track face identity embedding stability across frames.
    Real faces maintain consistent identity; deepfakes may drift.
    """

    def track(self, face_crops_sequence):
        """
        face_crops_sequence: list of (112, 112, 3) aligned face crops
        Returns: drift metrics
        """
        # Extract ArcFace embeddings per frame
        embeddings = []
        for crop in face_crops_sequence:
            emb = self.arcface(crop)  # (512,)
            emb = emb / np.linalg.norm(emb)
            embeddings.append(emb)
        embeddings = np.stack(embeddings)  # (T, 512)

        # Reference: mean embedding
        mean_emb = embeddings.mean(axis=0)
        mean_emb = mean_emb / np.linalg.norm(mean_emb)

        # Cosine similarity to reference over time
        similarities = embeddings @ mean_emb  # (T,)

        # Frame-to-frame similarity
        frame_sim = np.array([
            np.dot(embeddings[t], embeddings[t+1])
            for t in range(len(embeddings) - 1)
        ])

        features = {
            'mean_similarity': similarities.mean(),
            'min_similarity': similarities.min(),
            'similarity_std': similarities.std(),
            'drift_slope': np.polyfit(np.arange(len(similarities)), similarities, 1)[0],
            'frame_to_frame_sim_mean': frame_sim.mean(),
            'frame_to_frame_sim_min': frame_sim.min(),
            'n_identity_jumps': np.sum(frame_sim < 0.7),  # sudden identity changes
        }

        return features
```

### 5.2 Confidence-Weighted Fusion Implementation

```python
class EnsembleFusionEngine:
    """
    Fuses outputs from multiple independent detectors using
    learned confidence-weighted voting.
    """

    def __init__(self, n_detectors=6):
        # Learnable detector weights (trained on validation set)
        self.detector_weights = nn.Parameter(torch.ones(n_detectors))

        # Confidence calibration networks (one per detector)
        self.calibrators = nn.ModuleList([
            TemperatureScaling() for _ in range(n_detectors)
        ])

        # Meta-classifier: takes all detector outputs and makes final decision
        self.meta_classifier = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(n_detectors * 3, 128),  # score + confidence + features per detector
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Dropout(0.2),
            nn.Linear(128, 64),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Linear(64, 4)  # [real, AI-generated, AI-modified, partial]
        )

        # Disagreement analyzer
        self.disagreement_head = nn.Linear(n_detectors, 1)

    def fuse(self, detector_outputs: List[DetectorOutput]) -> FusionResult:
        """
        detector_outputs: list of (score, confidence, features) from each detector

        Fusion strategies combined:
        1. Weighted average (primary)
        2. Meta-classifier (secondary)
        3. Disagreement detection (safety check)
        """
        scores = torch.stack([d.score for d in detector_outputs])       # (N,)
        confidences = torch.stack([d.confidence for d in detector_outputs])  # (N,)
        features = torch.stack([d.features for d in detector_outputs])  # (N, D)

        # 1. Calibrate confidences
        cal_confidences = torch.stack([
            cal(conf) for cal, conf in zip(self.calibrators, confidences)
        ])

        # 2. Weighted average fusion
        weights = F.softmax(self.detector_weights, dim=0)
        weighted_score = (weights * cal_confidences * scores).sum() / \
                         (weights * cal_confidences).sum()

        # 3. Meta-classifier fusion
        meta_input = torch.cat([scores, cal_confidences, features.mean(dim=-1)], dim=0)
        meta_prediction = self.meta_classifier(meta_input.unsqueeze(0))

        # 4. Disagreement detection
        disagreement = self._compute_disagreement(scores, cal_confidences)

        # 5. Final decision
        if disagreement > 0.5:
            # High disagreement: rely more on meta-classifier, flag for review
            final_score = F.softmax(meta_prediction, dim=-1)
            needs_review = True
        else:
            # Low disagreement: use weighted average with meta-classifier blend
            avg_score = weighted_score
            meta_score = F.softmax(meta_prediction, dim=-1)[0, 1]  # P(fake)
            final_score = 0.6 * avg_score + 0.4 * meta_score
            needs_review = False

        return FusionResult(
            final_score=final_score,
            per_detector_scores=scores,
            per_detector_confidences=cal_confidences,
            disagreement=disagreement,
            needs_review=needs_review,
            meta_prediction=meta_prediction
        )

    def _compute_disagreement(self, scores, confidences):
        """Measure inter-detector disagreement"""
        # Weighted standard deviation of scores
        weights = confidences / confidences.sum()
        weighted_mean = (weights * scores).sum()
        weighted_var = (weights * (scores - weighted_mean) ** 2).sum()
        return torch.sqrt(weighted_var)
```

```python
            nn.Linear(256, 128)

            nn.Linear(512 + 128, 256),
            nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Dropout(0.3),
            nn.Linear(256, len(self.known_generators)),

        self.novelty_detector = GaussianMixtureModel(n_components=len(self.known_generators))

    def identify(self, image_or_frame):
        """
        Returns: (generator_name, confidence, is_known_generator)
        """
        # Extract noise residual fingerprint
        fp_features = self.fingerprint_net(image_or_frame)  # (512,)

        # Extract spectral fingerprint
        spectrum = self._compute_spectral_features(image_or_frame)  # (256,)
        spec_features = self.spectral_net(spectrum)  # (128,)

        # Combine
        combined = torch.cat([fp_features, spec_features])  # (640,)

        # Classify known generators
        logits = self.classifier(combined)
        probs = F.softmax(logits, dim=-1)
        top_generator = list(self.known_generators.keys())[probs.argmax()]
        top_confidence = probs.max().item()

        # Novelty detection: is this from an unknown generator?
        novelty_score = self.novelty_detector.score(combined.detach().numpy())
        is_known = novelty_score > self.novelty_threshold

        return {
            'generator': top_generator if is_known else 'Unknown',
            'confidence': top_confidence,
            'is_known_generator': is_known,
            'top_k_generators': self._top_k(probs, k=3),
            'fingerprint_embedding': combined  # for clustering/analysis
        }

    def _compute_spectral_features(self, image):
        """Extract azimuthally averaged power spectrum features"""
        gray = image.mean(dim=0) if image.dim() == 3 else image
        fft = torch.fft.fft2(gray)
        magnitude = torch.abs(torch.fft.fftshift(fft))

        # Azimuthal average
        h, w = magnitude.shape
        cy, cx = h // 2, w // 2
        max_r = min(cy, cx)

        radial_avg = torch.zeros(max_r)
        for r in range(max_r):
            mask = self._annular_mask(h, w, r, r+1)
            radial_avg[r] = magnitude[mask].mean()

        return radial_avg[:256]  # fixed-size feature
```

### 6.3 Open-Set Generator Detection

```python
class OpenSetGeneratorDetector:
    """
    Detects when a video comes from a PREVIOUSLY UNSEEN generator.
    Critical for future-proofing against new AI models.

    Uses: Mahalanobis distance in fingerprint embedding space
    """

    def __init__(self, known_generator_embeddings):
        # Fit class-conditional Gaussians to known generator embeddings
        self.class_means = {}
        self.class_covs = {}
        self.shared_cov_inv = None

        for gen_name, embeddings in known_generator_embeddings.items():
            self.class_means[gen_name] = embeddings.mean(axis=0)
            self.class_covs[gen_name] = np.cov(embeddings.T)

        # Shared covariance (tied covariance model)
        all_embeddings = np.concatenate(list(known_generator_embeddings.values()))
        shared_cov = np.cov(all_embeddings.T)
        self.shared_cov_inv = np.linalg.inv(shared_cov + 1e-4 * np.eye(shared_cov.shape[0]))

    def detect_novel_generator(self, embedding):
        """
        Compute Mahalanobis distance to all known generator classes.
        If minimum distance exceeds threshold, flag as unknown generator.
        """
        min_distance = float('inf')
        closest_known = None

        for gen_name, mean in self.class_means.items():
            diff = embedding - mean
            distance = np.sqrt(diff @ self.shared_cov_inv @ diff)
            if distance < min_distance:
                min_distance = distance
                closest_known = gen_name

        is_novel = min_distance > self.novelty_threshold

        return {
            'is_novel_generator': is_novel,
            'closest_known_generator': closest_known,
            'mahalanobis_distance': min_distance,
            'novelty_confidence': 1 - np.exp(-min_distance / self.scale)
        }
```

#### 7.2.1 Adversarial Training

```python
class AdversarialTrainer:
    """
    Train Glass AI detector with adversarial examples mixed in.
    Uses PGD-AT (Projected Gradient Descent Adversarial Training).
    """

    def adversarial_training_step(self, model, batch, optimizer):
        images, labels = batch

        # 1. Generate adversarial examples (PGD-7)
        adv_images = self.pgd_attack(
            model=model,
            images=images,
            labels=labels,
            epsilon=8/255,      # L∞ budget
            step_size=2/255,    # per-step perturbation
            num_steps=7,        # PGD iterations
            random_start=True
        )

        # 2. Mix clean and adversarial examples (50/50)
        mixed_images = torch.cat([images, adv_images])
        mixed_labels = torch.cat([labels, labels])

        # 3. Forward pass on mixed batch
        predictions = model(mixed_images)
        loss = F.cross_entropy(predictions, mixed_labels)

        # 4. Update
        optimizer.zero_grad()
        loss.backward()
        optimizer.step()

        return loss.item()

    def pgd_attack(self, model, images, labels, epsilon, step_size, num_steps, random_start):
        """Projected Gradient Descent attack"""
        adv = images.clone().detach()

        if random_start:
            adv = adv + torch.empty_like(adv).uniform_(-epsilon, epsilon)
            adv = torch.clamp(adv, 0, 1)

        for _ in range(num_steps):
            adv.requires_grad_(True)
            outputs = model(adv)
            loss = F.cross_entropy(outputs, labels)
            grad = torch.autograd.grad(loss, adv)[0]

            adv = adv.detach() + step_size * grad.sign()
            perturbation = torch.clamp(adv - images, -epsilon, epsilon)
            adv = torch.clamp(images + perturbation, 0, 1)

        return adv.detach()
```

#### 7.2.2 Input Preprocessing Defenses

```python
class RobustPreprocessor:
    """
    Apply preprocessing that neutralizes adversarial perturbations
    while preserving forensic artifacts.
    """

    def __init__(self):
        self.transforms = [
            # JPEG compression (destroys high-freq adversarial perturbations)
            ('jpeg_compress', lambda x: jpeg_compress_decompress(x, quality=85)),

            # Gaussian smoothing (mild, preserves macro artifacts)
            ('mild_blur', lambda x: gaussian_blur(x, sigma=0.5)),

            # Bit-depth reduction (quantizes away small perturbations)
            ('bit_reduce', lambda x: (x * 16).round() / 16),

            # Random resize (breaks pixel-aligned attacks)
            ('random_resize', lambda x: resize(x,
                scale=np.random.uniform(0.9, 1.1),
                interpolation='bilinear')),

            # Color jitter
            ('color_jitter', lambda x: color_jitter(x,
                brightness=0.05, contrast=0.05, saturation=0.05)),
        ]

    def defend(self, image, n_augmentations=5):
        """
        Apply random subset of preprocessing transforms.
        Run detector on multiple augmented versions and average predictions.
        """
        predictions = []
        for _ in range(n_augmentations):
            # Random subset of transforms
            subset = random.sample(self.transforms, k=random.randint(1, 3))
            augmented = image.copy()
            for name, transform in subset:
                augmented = transform(augmented)
            predictions.append(self.detector(augmented))

        # Median prediction (robust to outliers from adversarial manipulation)
        return np.median(predictions, axis=0)
```

```python
class FeatureSqueezingDefense:
    """
    Detect adversarial examples by comparing predictions on
    original vs. 'squeezed' (simplified) inputs.
    Large disagreement indicates adversarial manipulation.
    """

    def detect_adversarial(self, image, model):
        # Original prediction
        pred_orig = model(image)

        # Squeezed predictions
        squeezed_versions = [
            jpeg_compress(image, quality=75),
            bit_reduce(image, bits=5),
            median_filter(image, kernel=3),
            gaussian_blur(image, sigma=1.0),
        ]

        pred_squeezed = [model(sq) for sq in squeezed_versions]

        # Compare: large difference → likely adversarial
        max_diff = max([
            F.kl_div(pred_orig.log(), sq, reduction='sum').item()
            for sq in pred_squeezed
        ])

        is_adversarial = max_diff > self.threshold
        return is_adversarial, max_diff
```

```python
class MultiScaleDefense:
    """
    Analyze video at multiple scales.
    Adversarial perturbations are scale-dependent and break at different resolutions.
    Forensic artifacts are scale-invariant.
    """

    def multi_scale_detect(self, video_frames, model):
        scales = [1.0, 0.75, 0.5, 1.25, 1.5]  # relative to original
        predictions_per_scale = []

        for scale in scales:
            resized = [resize(f, scale=scale) for f in video_frames]
            # Re-extract to original size for model input
            normalized = [resize(f, target_size=(224, 224)) for f in resized]
            pred = model(torch.stack(normalized))
            predictions_per_scale.append(pred)

        # Consistency check
        pred_std = torch.stack(predictions_per_scale).std(dim=0)

        # Robust aggregation: if predictions are consistent → trust average
        # If inconsistent → flag as potentially adversarial
        if pred_std.mean() < self.consistency_threshold:
            return torch.stack(predictions_per_scale).mean(dim=0)
        else:
            return {
                'prediction': torch.stack(predictions_per_scale).median(dim=0).values,
                'adversarial_flag': True,
                'scale_inconsistency': pred_std
            }
```

```python
class DiverseEnsembleDefense:
    """
    Maintain ensemble of architecturally diverse detectors.
    Different architectures have different adversarial vulnerability surfaces.
    An attack optimized for one architecture is less effective against others.
    """

    def __init__(self):
        self.detectors = [
            ViTDetector(patch_size=14),        # Vision Transformer
            ViTDetector(patch_size=16),        # Different patch size
            EfficientNetDetector('b3'),         # CNN-based
            ResNetDetector(depth=50),           # Different CNN
            SRMNetDetector(),                   # Noise residual based
            FrequencyDomainDetector(),          # Frequency domain only
        ]

    def detect(self, video):
        """
        Run all detectors. Even if adversarial attack fools some,
        architectural diversity ensures others remain robust.
        """
        predictions = [d(video) for d in self.detectors]

        # Majority vote with confidence weighting
        votes_fake = sum(1 for p in predictions if p['label'] == 'fake')
        votes_real = sum(1 for p in predictions if p['label'] == 'real')

        # Require supermajority (> 2/3) for definitive classification
        total = len(predictions)
        if votes_fake > total * 2/3:
            return 'fake', np.mean([p['confidence'] for p in predictions if p['label'] == 'fake'])
        elif votes_real > total * 2/3:
            return 'real', np.mean([p['confidence'] for p in predictions if p['label'] == 'real'])
        else:
            return 'uncertain', 0.5  # Trigger manual review
```

### 8.2 Self-Supervised Pre-training

```python
class ForensicContrastiveLearning:
    """
    Self-supervised pre-training using contrastive learning adapted
    for forensic feature extraction.

    Key insight: Different augmentations of the SAME video should produce
    similar forensic features, while DIFFERENT videos (especially real vs. fake)
    should produce dissimilar features.
    """

    def __init__(self, backbone, projection_dim=256, temperature=0.07):
        self.backbone = backbone
        self.projector = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(1024, 512), nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Linear(512, projection_dim)
        )
        self.temperature = temperature

    def contrastive_loss(self, batch):
        """
        InfoNCE loss with forensic-aware augmentations.
        """
        images = batch['images']  # (B, C, H, W)
        B = images.shape[0]

        # Create two views with forensic-preserving augmentations
        view1 = self.forensic_augment(images)
        view2 = self.forensic_augment(images)

        # Extract features
        z1 = self.projector(self.backbone(view1))  # (B, 256)
        z2 = self.projector(self.backbone(view2))  # (B, 256)

        # Normalize
        z1 = F.normalize(z1, dim=1)
        z2 = F.normalize(z2, dim=1)

        # InfoNCE loss
        logits = torch.mm(z1, z2.T) / self.temperature  # (B, B)
        labels = torch.arange(B, device=logits.device)

        loss = (F.cross_entropy(logits, labels) +
                F.cross_entropy(logits.T, labels)) / 2

        return loss

    def forensic_augment(self, images):
        """
        Augmentations that PRESERVE forensic artifacts while
        varying non-forensic aspects.
        """
        augmented = images.clone()
        for i in range(len(augmented)):
            if random.random() < 0.3:
                augmented[i] = random_crop_resize(augmented[i], scale=(0.8, 1.0))
            if random.random() < 0.3:
                augmented[i] = color_jitter(augmented[i], 0.1, 0.1, 0.1, 0.05)
            if random.random() < 0.2:
                augmented[i] = horizontal_flip(augmented[i])
            # NOTE: Avoid augmentations that destroy forensic signals:
            # NO heavy blur, NO JPEG compression, NO noise addition
        return augmented
```

### 8.3 Multi-Task Supervised Training

```python
class MultiTaskTrainer:
    """
    Joint training on multiple forensic tasks.

    Tasks:
    1. Binary authenticity (real vs. fake) — primary
    2. 4-class classification (real, generated, modified, partial)
    3. Generator identification (which model made it)
    4. Manipulation segmentation (where is the manipulation)
    5. Audio-visual sync (is audio matched to video)
    """

    def __init__(self, model, task_weights=None):
        self.model = model
        self.task_weights = task_weights or {
            'authenticity': 1.0,
            'classification': 0.8,
            'generator_id': 0.3,
            'segmentation': 0.5,
            'av_sync': 0.4,
            'rppg_reconstruction': 0.2,
        }

    def compute_loss(self, predictions, targets):
        losses = {}

        # Task 1: Binary authenticity
        losses['authenticity'] = F.binary_cross_entropy_with_logits(
            predictions['authenticity'], targets['is_fake'].float()
        )

        # Task 2: 4-class classification
        losses['classification'] = F.cross_entropy(
            predictions['classification'], targets['class_label']
        )

        # Task 3: Generator ID (only for fake samples)
        fake_mask = targets['is_fake'].bool()
        if fake_mask.any():
            losses['generator_id'] = F.cross_entropy(
                predictions['generator_id'][fake_mask],
                targets['generator_label'][fake_mask]
            )

        # Task 4: Manipulation segmentation
        if 'manipulation_map' in targets:
            losses['segmentation'] = F.binary_cross_entropy(
                predictions['manipulation_map'],
                targets['manipulation_map']
            )

        # Task 5: AV sync
        if 'av_sync_label' in targets:
            losses['av_sync'] = F.binary_cross_entropy(
                predictions['av_sync_score'],
                targets['av_sync_label'].float()
            )

        # Task 6: rPPG reconstruction (self-supervised, real videos only)
        real_mask = ~targets['is_fake'].bool()
        if real_mask.any() and 'rppg_gt' in targets:
            losses['rppg_reconstruction'] = F.mse_loss(
                predictions['rppg_signal'][real_mask],
                targets['rppg_gt'][real_mask]
            )

        # Weighted sum
        total_loss = sum(
            self.task_weights.get(k, 0) * v for k, v in losses.items()
        )

        return total_loss, losses
```

### 8.4 Contrastive Learning for Forensic Features

```python
class ForensicSupConLoss(nn.Module):
    """
    Supervised Contrastive Loss adapted for forensic detection.

    Pulls together embeddings of:
    - Real videos (cluster of "real")
    - Videos from the same generator (cluster per generator)

    Pushes apart embeddings of:
    - Real vs. fake
    - Different generators
    """

    def __init__(self, temperature=0.1):
        super().__init__()
        self.temperature = temperature

    def forward(self, features, labels):
        """
        features: (B, D) — normalized embeddings
        labels: (B,) — class labels (0=real, 1=genA, 2=genB, ...)
        """
        B = features.shape[0]
        features = F.normalize(features, dim=1)

        # Similarity matrix
        sim = torch.mm(features, features.T) / self.temperature  # (B, B)

        # Mask: positive pairs share same label
        labels_eq = labels.unsqueeze(0) == labels.unsqueeze(1)  # (B, B)
        mask_pos = labels_eq.float() - torch.eye(B, device=features.device)

        # Log-sum-exp over negatives
        exp_sim = torch.exp(sim)
        log_prob = sim - torch.log(exp_sim.sum(dim=1, keepdim=True) + 1e-8)

        # Mean of positive log-probs
        mean_log_pos = (mask_pos * log_prob).sum(dim=1) / (mask_pos.sum(dim=1) + 1e-8)

        loss = -mean_log_pos.mean()
        return loss
```

### 8.5 Continual Learning

```python
class ContinualLearningTrainer:
    """
    Adapts detector to new generators without catastrophic forgetting.

    Strategy: Elastic Weight Consolidation (EWC) + Experience Replay

    When a new generator appears in the wild:
    1. Collect samples from the new generator
    2. Compute Fisher Information Matrix on old tasks
    3. Train on new data with EWC penalty
    4. Add replay samples from old generators to each batch
    """

    def __init__(self, model, ewc_lambda=5000, replay_ratio=0.3):
        self.model = model
        self.ewc_lambda = ewc_lambda
        self.replay_ratio = replay_ratio
        self.replay_buffer = ReplayBuffer(max_size=100000)
        self.fisher_matrices = {}
        self.optimal_params = {}

    def register_task(self, task_name, dataloader):
        """After training on a task, store Fisher info and optimal params"""
        fisher = self._compute_fisher(dataloader)
        self.fisher_matrices[task_name] = fisher
        self.optimal_params[task_name] = {
            n: p.clone().detach() for n, p in self.model.named_parameters()
        }

    def train_new_generator(self, new_data, new_label):
        """Train to detect a new generator while preserving old knowledge"""
        for batch in new_data:
            images, labels = batch

            # Mix in replay samples
            replay_batch = self.replay_buffer.sample(
                int(len(images) * self.replay_ratio)
            )
            if replay_batch:
                images = torch.cat([images, replay_batch['images']])
                labels = torch.cat([labels, replay_batch['labels']])

            # Forward pass
            predictions = self.model(images)
            task_loss = F.cross_entropy(predictions, labels)

            # EWC penalty: penalize moving away from optimal params for old tasks
            ewc_loss = 0
            for task_name, fisher in self.fisher_matrices.items():
                for n, p in self.model.named_parameters():
                    if n in fisher:
                        ewc_loss += (fisher[n] * (p - self.optimal_params[task_name][n]) ** 2).sum()

            total_loss = task_loss + self.ewc_lambda * ewc_loss

            self.optimizer.zero_grad()
            total_loss.backward()
            self.optimizer.step()

            # Add to replay buffer
            self.replay_buffer.add(images[:len(batch[0])], labels[:len(batch[1])])

    def _compute_fisher(self, dataloader, n_samples=2000):
        """Compute diagonal Fisher Information Matrix"""
        fisher = {n: torch.zeros_like(p) for n, p in self.model.named_parameters()}
        self.model.eval()

        for i, (images, labels) in enumerate(dataloader):
            if i * images.shape[0] >= n_samples:
                break
            self.model.zero_grad()
            output = self.model(images)
            loss = F.cross_entropy(output, labels)
            loss.backward()

            for n, p in self.model.named_parameters():
                if p.grad is not None:
                    fisher[n] += p.grad.data ** 2

        for n in fisher:
            fisher[n] /= n_samples

        return fisher
```

```python
                'FPR@TPR=0.99': self._fpr_at_tpr(labels, predictions[:, 1], tpr=0.99),

                # 6. Equal Error Rate
                'EER': self._compute_eer(labels, predictions[:, 1]),

                # 7. Average Precision
                'AP': average_precision_score(labels, predictions[:, 1]),

                # 8. Calibration (ECE — Expected Calibration Error)
                'ECE': self._expected_calibration_error(
                    labels, predictions[:, 1], confidences, n_bins=15
                ),

                # 9. Partial AUC (low FPR region — most practical)
                'pAUC@FPR=0.1': roc_auc_score(labels, predictions[:, 1],
                                               max_fpr=0.1),
            }

        return results

    def _fpr_at_tpr(self, labels, scores, tpr):
        """False positive rate when true positive rate = tpr"""
        fpr_vals, tpr_vals, _ = roc_curve(labels, scores)
        idx = np.searchsorted(tpr_vals, tpr)
        return fpr_vals[min(idx, len(fpr_vals) - 1)]

    def _compute_eer(self, labels, scores):
        """Equal Error Rate: where FPR = FNR"""
        fpr, tpr, thresholds = roc_curve(labels, scores)
        fnr = 1 - tpr
        idx = np.nanargmin(np.abs(fpr - fnr))
        return (fpr[idx] + fnr[idx]) / 2
```

```python
class CrossGeneratorEvaluator:
    """
    Leave-one-generator-out evaluation.
    Tests whether the detector generalizes to unseen generators.
    """

    def evaluate_generalization(self, model_factory, full_dataset, generators):
        results = {}

        for held_out_gen in generators:
            # Train on all generators except held-out
            train_generators = [g for g in generators if g != held_out_gen]
            train_data = full_dataset.filter(generators=train_generators + ['real'])
            test_data = full_dataset.filter(generators=[held_out_gen, 'real'])

            # Train fresh model
            model = model_factory()
            train(model, train_data)

            # Evaluate on held-out generator
            auc = evaluate_auc(model, test_data)
            results[held_out_gen] = {
                'AUC': auc,
                'is_generalizable': auc > 0.85  # threshold for "good" generalization
            }

        results['mean_cross_gen_AUC'] = np.mean([r['AUC'] for r in results.values()])
        return results
```

```python
class GlassExplainer:
    """
    Produces human-interpretable explanations for detection decisions.
    Three levels of explanation:
    1. Visual: Heatmaps showing WHERE manipulation is detected
    2. Numeric: Confidence scores per artifact type
    3. Textual: Natural language explanation of findings

    def explain(self, video_path, detection_result):

        # === LEVEL 1: Visual Heatmaps ===
        explanation['heatmaps'] = self.generate_heatmaps(video_path, detection_result)

        # === LEVEL 2: Numeric Scores ===
        explanation['scores'] = {
            'overall_authenticity': detection_result.classification[0].item(),
            'overall_fake_probability': detection_result.classification[1].item(),
            'confidence': detection_result.confidence.item(),

            'spatial_artifacts': {
                'face_geometry_anomaly': detection_result.artifact_types[0].item(),
                'skin_texture_anomaly': detection_result.artifact_types[1].item(),
                'boundary_artifact': detection_result.artifact_types[2].item(),
                'frequency_anomaly': detection_result.artifact_types[3].item(),
                'eye_anomaly': detection_result.artifact_types[4].item(),
            },

            'temporal_signals': {
                'identity_drift': detection_result.identity_drift.std().item(),
                'temporal_coherence': detection_result.coherence.item(),
                'rppg_quality': self._evaluate_rppg(detection_result.rppg_signal),
                'blink_naturalness': detection_result.blink_analysis['blink_rate_per_min'],
            },

            'audio_visual': {
                'av_sync_score': detection_result.av_sync_score.item(),
                'voice_authenticity': detection_result.voice_type[0].item(),
            },

            'identity': {
                'consistency': detection_result.identity_consistency.item(),
                'reference_match': getattr(detection_result, 'reference_match', None),
            },

            'generator_prediction': {
                'most_likely': detection_result.generator_id.argmax().item(),
                'confidence': detection_result.generator_id.max().item(),
            }
        }

        # === LEVEL 3: Natural Language Explanation ===
        explanation['text'] = self.generate_text_explanation(explanation['scores'])

        return explanation

    def generate_heatmaps(self, video_path, result):
        """
        Generate per-frame manipulation heatmaps using Grad-CAM
        and manipulation segmentation maps.
        """
        heatmaps = {}

        # 1. Grad-CAM heatmap (which regions drive the "fake" prediction)
        gradcam = GradCAM(model=self.model.spatial_detector,
                          target_layers=[self.model.spatial_detector.backbone.blocks[-1]])

        frames = decode_video_frames(video_path, n_frames=16)
        for i, frame in enumerate(frames):
            cam = gradcam(frame.unsqueeze(0))
            heatmaps[f'gradcam_frame_{i}'] = cam

        # 2. Manipulation segmentation map (from spatial detector)
        manip_map = result.manipulation_map  # (14, 14) per frame
        manip_map_upscaled = F.interpolate(
            manip_map.unsqueeze(0).unsqueeze(0),
            size=(224, 224), mode='bilinear'
        ).squeeze()
        heatmaps['manipulation_segmentation'] = manip_map_upscaled

        # 3. Frequency anomaly map
        freq_map = self._compute_frequency_anomaly_map(frames[0])
        heatmaps['frequency_anomaly'] = freq_map

        # 4. Identity drift timeline
        heatmaps['identity_drift_timeline'] = result.identity_drift  # (T,)

        # 5. rPPG signal visualization
        heatmaps['rppg_signal'] = result.rppg_signal  # (T,)

        return heatmaps

    def generate_text_explanation(self, scores):
        """Generate human-readable explanation"""
        explanations = []

        fake_prob = scores['overall_fake_probability']
        if fake_prob > 0.8:
            explanations.append(f"This video is highly likely AI-generated or manipulated "
                              f"(confidence: {fake_prob:.1%}).")
        elif fake_prob > 0.5:
            explanations.append(f"This video shows signs of possible manipulation "
                              f"(confidence: {fake_prob:.1%}).")
        else:
            explanations.append(f"This video appears to be authentic "
                              f"(authenticity confidence: {scores['overall_authenticity']:.1%}).")

        # Spatial artifacts
        spatial = scores['spatial_artifacts']
        if spatial['face_geometry_anomaly'] > 0.7:
            explanations.append("Facial geometry shows abnormal symmetry or proportions.")
        if spatial['skin_texture_anomaly'] > 0.7:
            explanations.append("Skin texture appears artificially smooth or has synthetic noise patterns.")
        if spatial['boundary_artifact'] > 0.7:
            explanations.append("Visible blending boundary detected around the face region.")
        if spatial['frequency_anomaly'] > 0.7:
            explanations.append("Frequency spectrum analysis reveals non-natural patterns consistent "
                              "with AI generation.")
        if spatial['eye_anomaly'] > 0.7:
            explanations.append("Eye reflections or pupil characteristics are inconsistent.")

        # Temporal
        temporal = scores['temporal_signals']
        if temporal['identity_drift'] > 0.1:
            explanations.append("Face identity drifts across frames, suggesting frame-by-frame generation.")
        if temporal['rppg_quality'] < 0.3:
            explanations.append("No biological pulse signal detected — real human faces show subtle "
                              "skin color changes from blood flow.")
        if temporal['blink_naturalness'] < 5 or temporal['blink_naturalness'] > 30:
            explanations.append(f"Blink rate ({temporal['blink_naturalness']:.0f}/min) is outside "
                              f"the normal human range (15-20/min).")

        # Audio-visual
        av = scores['audio_visual']
        if av['av_sync_score'] < 0.5:
            explanations.append("Audio and lip movements are poorly synchronized.")
        if av['voice_authenticity'] < 0.5:
            explanations.append("Voice shows characteristics of AI synthesis or cloning.")

        # Generator identification
        gen = scores['generator_prediction']
        if fake_prob > 0.7 and gen['confidence'] > 0.6:
            gen_name = self.generator_names[gen['most_likely']]
            explanations.append(f"The manipulation signature most closely matches: {gen_name}.")

        return " ".join(explanations)
```

```python
class AttentionVisualizer:
    """
    Visualize where the model attends for each modality.
    Shows which visual patches, audio segments, and temporal frames
    are most important for the decision.
    """

    def visualize_cross_modal_attention(self, model, inputs):
        """Extract and visualize attention patterns from fusion transformer"""
        # Hook into cross-modal attention layers
        attention_maps = {}

        def hook_fn(name):
            def fn(module, input, output):
                if hasattr(output, 'attn_weights'):
                    attention_maps[name] = output.attn_weights.detach()
            return fn

        # Register hooks
        for i, layer in enumerate(model.fusion.fusion_layers):
            layer.cross_attn.register_forward_hook(hook_fn(f'fusion_layer_{i}'))

        # Forward pass
        with torch.no_grad():
            result = model(inputs)

        # Visualize: which audio tokens attend to which visual tokens
        # This reveals audio-visual alignment patterns
        return attention_maps
```

```python
class ModelOptimizer:
    """Optimize Glass AI model for production deployment"""

    def optimize_for_inference(self, model):
        """
        Apply inference optimizations:
        1. TensorRT conversion for GPU inference
        2. ONNX export for cross-platform compatibility
        3. Quantization for edge deployment
        4. Batched inference for throughput
        """
        optimizations = {}

        # 1. TorchScript compilation
        scripted = torch.jit.script(model)
        optimizations['torchscript'] = scripted

        # 2. ONNX export
        dummy_input = {
            'frames': torch.randn(1, 16, 3, 224, 224),
            'audio': torch.randn(1, 16000 * 10),
        }
        torch.onnx.export(model, dummy_input, "glass_ai.onnx",
                         opset_version=17,
                         dynamic_axes={'frames': {0: 'batch', 1: 'n_frames'},
                                      'audio': {0: 'batch', 1: 'length'}})
        optimizations['onnx'] = "glass_ai.onnx"

        # 3. TensorRT optimization
        import tensorrt as trt
        trt_engine = self._build_trt_engine("glass_ai.onnx",
                                             fp16=True,
                                             max_batch=32)
        optimizations['tensorrt'] = trt_engine

        # 4. INT8 quantization for edge/mobile
        quantized = torch.quantization.quantize_dynamic(
            model, {nn.Linear, nn.Conv2d}, dtype=torch.qint8
        )
        optimizations['quantized'] = quantized

        return optimizations

    def benchmark(self, model, input_size, n_iterations=100):
        """Benchmark inference speed"""
        model.eval()
        dummy = torch.randn(*input_size).cuda()

        # Warmup
        for _ in range(10):
            with torch.no_grad():
                model(dummy)

        # Benchmark
        torch.cuda.synchronize()
        start = time.time()
        for _ in range(n_iterations):
            with torch.no_grad():
                model(dummy)
        torch.cuda.synchronize()
        elapsed = time.time() - start

        return {
            'avg_latency_ms': (elapsed / n_iterations) * 1000,
            'throughput_videos_per_sec': n_iterations / elapsed
```

### 11.6 Real-Time Detection Pipeline

```python
class RealTimeDetector:
    """
    Real-time detection for live video streams.
    Uses a sliding window approach with lightweight per-frame screening
    and periodic deep analysis.
    """

    def __init__(self, model, lightweight_model):
        self.full_model = model              # Full Glass AI (GPU)
        self.screen_model = lightweight_model  # EfficientNet-B0 (GPU, fast)
        self.frame_buffer = deque(maxlen=64)
        self.analysis_interval = 30  # deep analysis every 30 frames

    async def process_stream(self, video_stream):
        frame_count = 0
        current_verdict = "unknown"

        async for frame in video_stream:
            self.frame_buffer.append(frame)
            frame_count += 1

            # Lightweight screening every frame (< 5ms)
            quick_score = self.screen_model(frame)

            if quick_score > 0.7:  # suspicious
                # Immediate flag
                yield StreamAlert(
                    frame=frame_count,
                    type="suspicious_frame",
                    score=quick_score
                )

            # Deep analysis periodically
            if frame_count % self.analysis_interval == 0 and len(self.frame_buffer) >= 16:
                # Run full model on buffered frames (async, non-blocking)
                frames_batch = list(self.frame_buffer)[-16:]
                result = await asyncio.to_thread(
                    self.full_model.analyze_frames, frames_batch
                )
                current_verdict = result.verdict

                yield StreamAnalysis(
                    frame_range=(frame_count - 16, frame_count),
                    result=result
                )
```

## FUTURE IMPROVEMENTS

### Near-Term (6-12 months)

1. **3D Face Reconstruction Verification**: Use monocular 3D face reconstruction to verify geometric consistency. Real faces produce consistent 3D meshes from multiple angles; deepfakes produce inconsistent geometry.

2. **Neural Codec Forensics**: As neural video codecs (e.g., NVIDIA NVC) become common, develop forensic analysis for neural compression artifacts vs. generative artifacts.

3. **Watermark Detection**: Integrate detection of AI-watermarks (SynthID, C2PA) as an additional signal. Presence of watermarks confirms AI generation; absence doesn't confirm authenticity.

4. **Federated Learning**: Allow organizations to fine-tune the model on their specific video types without sharing sensitive data, using federated learning.

### Medium-Term (1-2 years)

5. **Multi-Language Voice Forensics**: Extend audio analysis to 50+ languages with language-specific phoneme-viseme models.

6. **Full-Body Deepfake Detection**: Extend beyond face to detect AI-generated full-body video, clothing manipulation, and background synthesis.

7. **Interactive Video Analysis**: AI-powered investigation tool where analysts can query specific aspects of a video and get detailed forensic analysis.

8. **Real-Time Video Call Protection**: Sub-100ms detection for protecting live video calls against real-time deepfake attacks.

### Long-Term (2-5 years)

9. **Foundation Model for Forensics**: Train a large-scale foundation model (billions of parameters) specifically for media forensics, capable of zero-shot detection of new manipulation types.

10. **Provenance Chain Verification**: Integrate with C2PA/Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity to verify the complete capture-to-display chain of custody.

11. **Quantum-Resistant Forensics**: As quantum computing may break current cryptographic provenance systems, develop forensic methods that remain valid regardless of cryptographic assumptions.

12. **Neuromorphic Detection**: Deploy detection on neuromorphic hardware for energy-efficient, always-on monitoring at the edge.
